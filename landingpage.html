AR/VR Headset Technology

Virtual Reality (VR) is a computer-generated environment that immerses users in a completely synthetic, digital experience. VR typically involves the use of a headset that covers the user's eyes, providing a fully immersive and isolated virtual world.

Types of VR:

Tethered VR: These VR systems are connected to a powerful computer or gaming console. Examples include the Oculus Rift, HTC Vive, and PlayStation VR.

Standalone VR: These headsets have all the necessary components built-in, eliminating the need for an external device. Examples include Oculus Quest and Lenovo Mirage Solo.

Mobile VR: VR experiences can be delivered using smartphones inserted into VR headsets. Examples include Google Cardboard and Samsung Gear VR.

Our Motive

Immersive Experience: VR creates a sense of presence, making users feel as if they are physically present in a computer-generated environment.

Complete Isolation: Users are usually isolated from the real world, allowing them to fully engage with the virtual environment.

Diverse Applications: VR is utilized in various fields, including gaming, education, healthcare, training simulations, virtual tourism, and therapy.

Devices: VR experiences are primarily delivered through VR headsets, which can be tethered to a computer or operate as standalone devices.
